Biotin

Also known as Vitamin B7, Biotin plays a crucial role in metabolic functions, particularly in the metabolism of fats and carbohydrates. It's essential for maintaining stable blood sugar levels and is often recommended for managing diabetes.

Cinnamon

Widely recognized for its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and anti-diabetic properties, Cinnamon is used to help reduce blood sugar spikes after meals. It improves insulin sensitivity and can play a role in the management of type 2 diabetes.

Zinc

An essential mineral, Zinc is crucial for immune function, protein synthesis, and DNA formation. It's also important for insulin production and function, making it a key component in managing blood sugar levels and diabetes.

Licorice

Known for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, Licorice root can influence blood sugar control and is believed to have anti-diabetic effects. It's also used for digestive health and can help in reducing sugar cravings.

Chromium

This essential trace mineral enhances the action of insulin and is important in carbohydrate, fat, and protein metabolism. Chromium supplementation can help improve blood sugar control in people with type 2 diabetes and insulin resistance.
8

Manganese

Manganese plays a vital role in the production of digestive enzymes responsible for a process called gluconeogenesis, which involves the conversion of protein's amino acids into sugar and the balance of sugar within the bloodstream.
9

Vitamin C

Vitamin Beyond its well-known role in immune function, Vitamin C has been shown to help lower blood sugar levels and manage blood pressure, making it a valuable component for overall metabolic health and diabetes management.

Vitamin E

Vitamin An antioxidant, Vitamin E helps in combating oxidative stress and inflammation, which are key factors in the development and progression of diabetes. It's also vital for immune function and cellular signaling.
